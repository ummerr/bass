## What it is

How to sit or stand with a bass so you can play for an hour without your
shoulder, wrist, or back complaining.

## Why it matters

Posture problems hide. They feel fine for 10 minutes and wreck you at 40.
Every other skill on this site assumes a neutral wrist — so getting the hold
right now saves unlearning later.

## How to practice

- **Strap it up**: even when sitting, wear the strap and adjust it so the
  bass sits in the same place standing or sitting. This keeps a single muscle
  memory.
- **Neck angle**: aim the headstock at ~10–2 o'clock, not straight out to the
  side. Reaching the first few frets should not require a bent left wrist.
- **Right-hand anchor**: rest your thumb on the pickup or the low E string.
  Stay relaxed; you are not gripping anything.
- **Check-in**: every few minutes drop your hands, shake them out, then
  return. If anything aches, something is wrong, not you getting stronger.

A mirror (or a phone propped up filming) is the fastest feedback loop.
