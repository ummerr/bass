## What it is

The default way to play bass: pluck with the tip of your index (i) and
middle (m) fingers, strictly alternating. Pick-based playing exists and is
fine, but fingerstyle is the foundational technique.

## Why it matters

Alternating fingers is what lets you play fast without tensing up. If you
pluck with the same finger twice in a row, your speed ceiling becomes that
one finger.

## How to practice

- Rest your thumb on the low E string (or the pickup).
- Pluck the open A string: i, m, i, m, i, m. Slowly. Count out loud.
- Every note is the same volume. That is the whole drill.
- Move to the D string, then G. Then walk back down.
- Once that is easy, alternate across two strings without breaking the i/m
  pattern: i on A, m on D, i on A, m on D.

Speed comes later. The first two weeks are about the pattern never breaking.
